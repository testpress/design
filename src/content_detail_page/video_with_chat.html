What is photosynthesis?
Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants convert sunlight into chemical energy using carbon dioxide and water.
Can you explain it in simpler terms?
Sure! It's like plants cooking their own food using sunlight, air, and water.
How long does the process take?
It's a continuous process as long as there is light, but the rate varies based on light intensity.
What about at night?
At night, many plants focus on "respiration" and the "dark reactions" (Calvin cycle) that don't need direct light.
Do all plants do this?
Almost all green plants, but some parasitic plants get their energy from hosts.
What is the Calvin cycle exactly?
It's the set of chemical reactions that take place in chloroplasts during photosynthesis, specifically the light-independent phase.
Is it important for humans too?
Absolutely! It produces the oxygen we breathe and the food we (or our food source) eat.
Where do the light reactions happen?
They happen in the thylakoid membranes of the chloroplasts.
