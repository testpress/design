How to read the revenue trend

This chart shows how revenue is changing over time. SVOD = subscriptions; TVOD = pay-per-view. Use the pattern below to interpret your current badge.

Pattern Chart Status What this means What to do
SVOD ↑ TVOD ↑ Good New users are subscribing and buying content. Maintain strategy and monitor consistency.
SVOD ↑ TVOD ↓ Good Users are choosing subscriptions over one-time purchases (good for long-term value). Continue pushing SVOD value.
SVOD ↓ TVOD ↑ Attention Users are paying, but avoiding subscriptions (price or commitment concern). Review SVOD pricing and entry plans.
SVOD → TVOD → Attention Growth has stopped (plateau). Check new content releases, marketing activity, and pricing tests.
SVOD → TVOD ↓ Attention Subscription growth is flat and purchase interest is falling. Improve content discovery and highlight high-value titles.
SVOD ↓ TVOD → Attention Subscription demand is weakening while purchases are stagnant. Focus on subscription value and retention.
SVOD ↓ TVOD ↓ Critical Both monetization models are declining. Review content quality, pricing, UX issues, and competitor activity immediately.
SVOD = 0 TVOD > 0 Attention Users buy content but no one subscribes. Introduce lower-entry SVOD plans or clarify subscription benefits.
SVOD > 0 TVOD = 0 Good Platform is working as a subscription-only business. Double down on SVOD. TVOD is optional.
SVOD = 0 TVOD = 0 Critical No new revenue in this period. If newly launched: verify setup (plans, payments, visibility). If live: urgently check payments, signup flow, marketing, and content availability.

How to read the churn card

This card shows how many subscribers are cancelling over time. Use the patterns below to understand what the churn trend means and what to do.

Pattern Chart Status What this means What to do
Churn ↓ (decreasing) Good Fewer users are cancelling. Retention is improving. Continue current content, pricing, and engagement strategy.
Churn → (stable) Attention Cancellations are steady. Losses are controlled but not improving. Look for retention improvements: onboarding, content reminders, engagement nudges.
Churn ↑ (gradual increase) Attention More users are cancelling than before. Early warning sign. Review recent content gaps, pricing perception, or value communication.
Churn ↑ sharply Critical Sudden increase in cancellations. User dissatisfaction likely increased. Focus on retention: content freshness, perceived value, plan benefits.
Churn high but flat Critical High cancellations have become consistent. Ongoing leakage. Revisit subscription value, plan structure, and long-term retention strategy.
Churn = 0 (early stage) Neutral No cancellations yet because subscriptions are still new. Monitor as the subscriber base matures. No action needed yet.
