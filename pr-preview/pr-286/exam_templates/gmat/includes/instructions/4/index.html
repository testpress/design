Differences between GMAT Official Practice Exams and the GMAT Exam

The tutorial, section instructions, exit screens and Report or Cancel Scores screens are not timed in the GMAT Official Practice Exams but WILL BE timed during the GMAT exam. You may want to send extra time on these screens in the Official Practice Exams to familiarize yourself with them before you take the actual test.

The optional break screens are not timed in the GMAT Official Practice Exams but will be timed when you sit for the exam. For the length of the optional breaks, refer to www.mba.com. Keep in mind that, if you exceed the time allowed for one of the optional breaks during the GMAT exam, the extra time will be deducted from the time available to complete the next section of exam material (the Quantitative, Verbal, or Integrated Reasoning section).

In the GMAT Official Practice Exams, you are permitted to move back and forth between the screens in the preliminary sections of the practice exams (the Welcome screen, the Nondisclosure Agreement, the school selection screen, and the tutorial). In the GMAT exam, you will not be able to return to a previous section once you have completed it.

The practice exams in the GMAT Official Practice platform contain a oFlag for Review to continue. feature that allows you to mark questions you may want to return to later. The oFlag for Review feature is not available during the GMAT exam.

The bSave for Later button is available in the Analytical Writing Assessment, Integrated Reasoning, Quantitative, and Verbal sections of the practice tests, but it is not available during the GMAT exam.

Please note:

If you are planning to take the GMAT online exam, then there are additional steps to be completed on your computer before exam day to help ensure that your computer is compatible, and you have a smooth testing experience.

For more information, please visit: System Requirements for the GMAT Online Exam on mba.com

The browser back button will not work during practice exams. Use the save for later option to navigate out of the exam.
