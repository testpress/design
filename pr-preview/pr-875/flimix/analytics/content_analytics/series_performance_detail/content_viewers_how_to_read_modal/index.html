How to read the series viewer activity section

This section shows how viewer engagement for this content is changing over time. Content viewers = number of users who watched this content. Watch time = total hours watched for this content. Use the patterns below to understand what the current badge means and what to do.

Pattern Chart Status What this means What to do
Content viewers ↑   Watch time ↑
Good More people are discovering and watching the content longer. Promote the content on homepage or recommendations to maintain momentum.
Content viewers ↑   Watch time ↓
Attention More users start the content but stop watching early. Check content quality, intro pacing, or thumbnail/title expectations.
Content viewers ↓   Watch time ↑
Attention Fewer new viewers are discovering it, but those who watch stay longer. Promote the content again to attract new viewers.
Content viewers →   Watch time →
Attention Engagement is stable with no growth. Refresh promotions or include the content in recommendation lists.
Content viewers →   Watch time ↓
Attention Same number of viewers but they spend less time watching. Review content quality or viewer retention points.
Content viewers ↓   Watch time →
Attention Audience discovery is dropping even though engagement is stable. Improve visibility through promotions or homepage placement.
Content viewers ↓   Watch time ↓
Critical Both discovery and engagement are falling. Re-promote the content, improve recommendations, or analyze viewer drop-off.
Content viewers = 0   Watch time > 0
Attention Data inconsistency — watch time recorded without any viewers. Check analytics tracking or reporting pipeline.
